Maker Johann Adolph Hass
Manufactured Hamburg, 1761
Description Unfretted German clavichord with Chinoiserie case on later stand.
Inscription J.A Hass / Hambr Anno 1761 on soundboard.
Specification Bichord brass stringing with octave strings from FF to c.
Pitch A=392
Compass FF-f3
References
Boalch3 number: HASS, J. A. 1761(4)
Boalch2 number: 17
Restoration 1980-81 Christoper Clarke (full restoration); Mac and Me (decoration)
1999 Miles Hellon (new strings and listing)
Maker Johann Heinrich Gräbner (the younger)
Manufactured Dresden, 1761
Description Small fretted travelling clavichord in oak case, with full width drawer below bottom-boards.
Inscription Johann Heinrich / Gräbner / Dresden 1761 Num 100 on underside of soundboard.
Specification Formerly strung as a quint instrument in bichord brass stringing with open wound strings in bass, recently restrung in iron and brass as an octave instrument; fretted from c, with d and a unfretted in each octave.
Pitch A=415
Compass C-e3
References
Boalch3 number: GRÄBNER, J. H. 1761.
Restoration 1982 Chris Nobbs
Maker Johann Gottlob Horn

Inscription Nom 321. / Johann Gottlob Horn. / Instrumentmacher. / in Dresden 1789.
Compass FF-g3
Restoration In progress: Chris Nobbs
Maker Johann Jacob Bodechtel
Manufactured Nürnberg, 1790s
Description Fretted German clavichord without stand.
Inscription Johann Jacob Bodechtel / Orgel und instrumenten macher / in Nürnberg in ink on paper label on bass hitchrail.
Specification Bichord brass stringing with open-wound strings in bass. Fretting, starting at d#/e, has unfretted d’s and a’s except for d3/d#3.
Pitch A=415
Compass C-f3
References
Boalch3 number: BODECHTEL, J. J. 1750 [sic]
Boalch2 number: 1.
Copies Copied (and expanded) by Peter Bavington as a 'Bavdechtel'.

Inscription Johann David Schiedmayer / Hochfûrstlish Anspachischer / Hof-instrumentenmacher / in Erlangen 1791 on printed paper label on bass hitchrail.
Specification Bichord iron and brass strings on divided bridge, with open wound strings in bass.

Maker Chickering / Arnold Dolmetsch
Manufactured Boston, 1909
Description Free copy of a five octave unfretted north German clavichord based on an instrument by Christian Gotthelf Hoffmann, Ronneburg, 1784. In dark green case with vermilion interior and raised on a turned trestle stand.
Inscriptions CHICKERING -&- SONS - BOSTON - VSA - MCMIX No 51. on nameboard, Made-by-Chickering-&-Sons-under-the-direction-of-Arnold-Dolmetsch Boston MCMIX No51 on the hitchplank, and PLUS FAIT DOUCEUR QUE VIOLENCE (“Gentleness does more than violence”, La Fontaine, Fables, VI/3) on the inside of the lid.
Specification Two gilt metal roses in soundboard. Large cast hinges of English harpsichord type. Unfretted and brass strung with covered strings in the bass.
Pitch A=440
Compass FF–f3
References Original in the Yale University Collection of Musical Instruments, No. 4945.60
(Boalch3 number: Hoffmann, C. G. 1784 (1))
Richard Troeger, 'The Dolmetsch/Chickering Clavichords and their model', De Clavicordio, II (1995).

Description Small clavichord in walnut casework on trestle stand with soundboard decorated with flowers. Built for Violet Gordon Woodhouse.

Specification Unfretted, bichord and strung in brass with close-wound bass strings.
Pitch A=440
Compass C-d3

Maker Adlam Burnett
Manufactured Finchcocks, Goudhurst, Kent, 1979
Description Copy of an early 19th century fretted clavichord by Georg Fredrich Schmahl, Ulm, 1807, in an unpainted coniferous case.
Inscription Adlam Burnett / Musical Instrument Makers / Goudhurst. Ao 1979 No 58 on a replica of an engraved Schmahl label.
Specification Bichord iron and brass stringing; in the bass from C to c one string of each pair is at the octave and the other is an open wound unison string. Fretted in equal temperament from g, with notes a and d unfretted in each octave (e3 and f3 a fretted pair).
Pitch A=415
Compass C-f3
References Original in the Collection of Richard Burnett at Finchcocks
(Boalch3 number: SCHMAHL, G. F. 1807).
